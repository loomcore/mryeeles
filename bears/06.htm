Grizzly bears

Characteristics

Grizzly bears are large brown creatures that have small round ears and pointy claws. They have 42 teeth. If a grizzly bear is living in the wild it will live for around 25 years. Bears can run up to 35 miles per hour. A cub would stay with thare mother for a many years beffor they went to live on thare own.

Habitat

Bears live in forests near softly flowing rivers. They only like to be around other bears of thare type. They live by rivers to catch fish for food. Bears can move through fast flowing rivers very easily. Bears are mostly found in America. The bears can come out the forest through the differant types of trees then catch thare prey with thare incredibly long muzzle.

Diet

Bears shred thare food with thare mouths before they eat it. They mainly eat fish and baby deer. They catch the fish by putting thare long muzzle into the water and incredibly quickly catch the fish. As a treat they eat nuts and berries. Bears can also eat the sap from inside the trees. Bears get the sap by using thare strong claws to break the bark on the tree. When bears are in the forest they sometimes will eat little insects. They eat around 13.5 kg a day.
