Facts about grizzly bears

Characteristics

Grizzly bears are large land animals that live in North America. Even though they are very large and can weigh up to 1,500 pounds they can run up to 40 mph. Even though they look very cute and cuddly they are the largest land animals and have very sharp canines. Most people think that grizzly bears can only be brown but they can be all sorts of colours including brown, black, gold and even white. Bears plumpen up in the summer to get ready for hibernation in the winter. They have 2 layers of skin and a dog like muzzle with big round ears on their head.

Diet

Grizzly bears survive on the fish that they catch in the river and they catch salmon to eat. They shred them up into small parts with their sharp claws. They need a river near their home to catch fish in but if they have to, they will eat berrys, insects and moths. Bears are omnivores because they eat berrys as well as fish.

Habitat

They live in North America in big forests near rivers. They live alone apart from with their cubs (baby bears) or mothers. They don't in the open for they find food in the forests. Bears usally hibernate in winter in caves to keep warm.
