Comb jellies

What are comb jellies?

Comb jellies look the same as jellyfish but they aren't. Comb jellies are invertabrates wich means they have no bones and they have to use the water to suport themselveles. A comb jellies scientific name is Ctenophora.

Characteristics

Comb jellies can harm fish very easly! Comb jellies are carnivores and are very dangerous! They are bioluminescent. They can open their mouth wide enough to eat a comb jelly or jellyfish whole! Some comb jellies can grow up to 1.5m and 8cm wide!Most comb jellies are neon! Some comb jellies grab their prey with their tentacles and drag their prey to their guts and digest their prey! A type of comb jelly is a cydippid (it is a spieces of comb jellies) wich are usely round or oval shaped. Comb jellies have pouches on each side of their mouth! Inside the mouth there are things called cilia that act like teeth. Comb jellies are called comb jellies because of the oar like combs that help them move throw the water. Inside their belly there are speciallised hooks to hold their prey in place.

Habitat

Most comb jellies live in open oceans, but some live on the coast of North America. Some comb jellies live under the ice in the arctic ocean! When the weather is bad,they go to the bottom of the ocean. Some live at the bottom of the ocean.

Some comb jellies live in Arctic ocean.

Predators and prey

Some turtles eat comb jellies! Tunafish, sunfish, butterfish and lots of other big fish eat comb jellies! Comb jellies eat lobsters crabs and other crustaceans. Comb jellies have special senses to track down food. Most comb jellies eat other comb jellies or jellyfish. Somtimes comb jellies eat tiny apertisers or a full corse meal. They open their mouth and keep the prey inside their bellies until their prey is digested!

No! Comb jellies live all over the world.
