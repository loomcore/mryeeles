Sea cucumbers

Characteristics

Sea cucumbers are related to star fish and sea urchins because they all have five gooey horns. There are 1,250 species of sea cucumber. They can go through super tight spaces, which is good beacause they can get away from pray. Sea cucumbers are in a group of sea animals called echinoderms. They move very slowly. Just because they move slowly it doesn't mean they are more endangered than other animals. The small sea cucumbers length is 0.12 inches the big sea cucumber is 3.9 feet. They are nocternal. Sea cucumbers look a bit like sea snakes. Sea cucumbers do not have brains. They can eat without a brain. They have poorly developed senses. Sea cucumbers have a sort of skeleton. They are a type of inverterbrate. Sea cucumbers release their babies into water. They look like cucumbers and they have soft bodies. They have interesting techniques when faced with danger. They have five tentacles.

Habitat

Sea cucumbers spend most of their life in the deep sea and in the rock pools. The rock pools have to be quite deep and have some good hiding places. Rarely sea cucumbers live in marinas. They live in the coral reefs. Sea cucumbers dont really have a proper house they just explore in the deep sea. Sea cucumbers normally live on or near the sea floor, sometimes buried beneath the sand.

Diet

Sea cucumbers eat the remains of dead animals, that sometimes live in the seafloor.Sea cucumbers sometimes eat particals what come down from the oceans surface. They eat with tube feet that surround their mouths.

Is it endangered?

Sea cucumbers are endangerded because they live in and near the coral reefs and there are lots of different fish and sea animals there that could eat them. They are also endangered because Japanese and Chinese people like to eat them.
