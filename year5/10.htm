DORMICE

CHARACTERISTICS

Dormice grow from 3 to 7.5 inches and weigh 15 to 30 grams. Their scientific name is Gliridae. The ancient Romens used to eat them. They live up to 5 or 6 years. They have large eyes, round ears, pink paws, fluffy tails and short legs. At the age of 4 weeks they can leave the nest and some Dormice are kept as pets. Dormice are also called sleeping mice and interestingly they are not mice. They are quite fat. Their tail is usally the same length as the body. Dormice have a chunky body and dormice like to climb trees.Dormice are a rodent, a rodent is a gnawing and nibbling animal.They have sharp elongated teeth. Dormice hibernate for around 6 months.

HABITAT

Dormice are found in Europe,Asia and Africa. In the UK you can find them in Scottland. You can also find them under ground and in grown hedgerows. They nest in holes in trees. They need to have grass and leaves.You can also find them in rocky places and in grasslands. to live. They need thick overgrown forests to live. The reason they live here is because it is easy to find food and homes. They also need it to be a bit damp. Damp is wet.

DIET

Dormice eat blackthorn a type of shrub tree and hornbeam which is a north american shrub tree. They eat some fruit, and they eat some flowers. They also eat hazel nuts and they eat nuts and they also eat some berries. Dormice nibble at their food.Dormice also bite their food.

Are Dormice Endangered?

Yes. Dormice are the most endangered mammals in Brittan because there homes have been lost. They search for food with other animals like grey squirels which are probably endangered to. More dormice are endangered like hazel dormice and some other ones.
