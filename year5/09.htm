The Black Rat

Characteristics:

The black rat's tail is 190-240mm long. Its weight is 140-280g. The black rat's lifespan is around 18 months. It has hairless ears and a hairless tail, the tail is longer than the head and the body put together, it is an omnivore. They are very agile climbers and are very good at swimming. Black rats travel in packs, their population is approximately 1,300 in Britain. There are over 80 species of rat. In the 14th century people found out that the black rat carried a disease called "The Black Death, " The black rat is smaller than the brown rat which is less dangerous.

Habitat:

In the tropics, black rats live in grassy fields. The black rat is closely associated with buildings around the world, although in Britain it tends to inhabit rocky shores and cliffs. The black rat is most often found in large numbers in coastal areas because of the way the species is spread through human sea faring. They also live in many aquatic areas.

Diet:

Black rats eat plants also humans' waste(thats rubbish, not poo and wee). They can also feast upon old fruit that has been thrown away like old banana skins and orange peels. They also eat grain from the fields. Not to mention titbits of ancient wheat and yucky, distusting bits of seed. The black rat's predators are domestic cats and gigantic birds like eagles. They eat by lifting the food to their mouth with their two front paws then nibbling the titbits in chunks.

Is it endangered?

The black rat is endangered in Britain, this is because they used to live in dockyards,but now they are all getting cleared away because they are old-fashioned.

Glossary:

Lifespan: How long it lives.

Omnivore: A animal that eats both veg and meat.

Population: The amount of people living in a area.

Aquatic: Areas surrounded with water.

Dockyards: A place where ships enter an exit.
